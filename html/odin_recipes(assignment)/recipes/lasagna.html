lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that consists of layers of pasta and cheese. It is typically made with tomato sauce and a variety of cheeses such as ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. The dish is baked in the oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the pasta is cooked through. Lasagna is a hearty and comforting meal that is perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cans (6 ounces each) tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked and drained

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the onion and garlic over medium heat until softened. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, sugar, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the mozzarella cheese, ricotta cheese, and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Spread a thin layer of the tomato sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Layer 4 lasagna noodles on top of the sauce. Spread one-third of the cheese mixture over the noodles. Repeat the layers two more times, ending with a layer of sauce on top.
  5. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before serving.
